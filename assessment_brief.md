Assessment OverviewModule Title: Software Testing (DAT5007)   Assessment Title: Software Testing and Design Written
Report (WRIT1)   Weighting: 75% Word Count: 6,000 words (including text, tables, figures, and citations; excluding
reference lists and appendices)   Pass Mark: 40% (Undergraduate) / 50% (Postgraduate)   Task DescriptionThe student must
conduct software testing and design for a sample case study of their choice (system, programs, or classes). The project
encompasses case study analysis, test plan development, test case design, execution, coverage analysis, and error
reporting. Required Report StructureIntroduction: Provide an overview of the software testing process, its phases, and
the goal of the report. Case Study: Explain the rationale for selecting the system. Describe its functionalities and
requirements (both functional and non-functional) and include descriptive screenshots of the interface. Testing Plan:
Develop a plan covering:Scope: Boundaries of testing. Types: White Box (unit/integration) and Black Box (system)
testing. Focus: Functional, performance, and security testing. Techniques & Tools: Specify methods like Equivalence
Partitioning or Boundary Value Analysis, and the IDE or framework utilized. Test Case Design: Design detailed cases for
unit, integration, and system levels. Include:A tabular list of specifications for test cases. Justification for the
methods used to design these cases. Test Execution: Describe the execution of all planned tests (including use of
drivers and stubs for integration). Present a comprehensive report with Pass/Fail outcomes, defect descriptions, and a
coverage report. Conclusion: Summarize key findings and the effectiveness of the testing process. Marking Criteria (100
Marks Total)Introduction (5%): Quality of the overview of the testing process and phases. Case Study (10%): Clarity of
system description, requirements, rationale, and screenshots. Testing Plan (15%): Quality of the scope, types,
techniques, and tool specifications. Test Case Design (30%): Excellence in the tabular specifications and justification
of techniques. Test Execution (30%): Detail in the execution description, Pass/Fail reporting, defect analysis, and
coverage. Conclusion (5%): Depth of insight and summary of relevant points. Writing and References (5%): Adherence to
formatting (Arial 12pt, 1.5 spacing), grammar, and citation standards.
